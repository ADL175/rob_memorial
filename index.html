Army Ranger Sgt. Robert D. Sanchez

Lead the Way Fund Sua Sponte Foundation Memorial U.S. Mountain Ranger
  • July 19, 1985 - Roberto "Rob" D. Sanchez, was born in Miami, FL.
  • July 2004 - Rob graduated from Satellite High School.
  • Late-July 2004 - Shortly after graduation, Rob enlisted in the U.S. Army.
  • January 2005 - Rob excelled in basic training, Airborne School and Ranger Indoctrination Program (RIP).
  • February 2005 - Robert was assigned to Company B, 1st Bn., 75th Ranger Regiment. He served as a team leader while assigned to 1st Battalion.
  • 2009 - Rob was on his fifth deployment, having been to Afghanistan once previously and having been to Iraq three times.
  • October 1, 2009 - Rob was killed during a combat operation involving multiple enemy contacts, killing nine enemy combatants and destroying a large weapons cache in Kandahar Province, Afghanistan.
  • Sgt. Sanchez's awards include:
    • Ranger Tab
    • Combat Infantrymans Badge
    • Expert Infantrymans Badge
    • Parachutist Badge
    • Army Commendation Medal
    • Joint Service Achievement Medal
    • Army Good Conduct Medal
    • National Defense Service Medal
    • Afghanistan Campaign Medal with combat star
    • Iraq Campaign Medal with combat star
    • Global War on Terrorism Service Medal
    • and Army Service Ribbon
  • He is survived by his parents, 1st Sgt. Will and Wendy Holland; brothers, Jacob Goldberg and Logan Holland of Hendersonville, TN.
Army Sgt. Robert Sanchez left a legacy of leadership, loyalty and living “his life for others.”
